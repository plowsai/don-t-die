Mental Health Resources

Help is available. If you or someone you know is struggling, you are not alone.

In Crisis? Get Immediate Help

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call 911 right away.

If you're having suicidal thoughts, call the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 988 or 1-800-273-8255 (available 24/7).

Crisis Hotlines

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline

    Call 988 or 1-800-273-8255

    Available 24/7

  • Crisis Text Line

    Text HOME to 741741

    Available 24/7

  • Veterans Crisis Line

    Call 988 then press 1 or 1-800-273-8255 (press 1)

    For veterans and military personnel

Self-Care Strategies

Physical Activity

Regular exercise can reduce stress and improve mood. Even a short daily walk can help.

Proper Sleep

Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep. Establish a regular sleep schedule and relaxing bedtime routine.

Healthy Eating

A balanced diet can support brain health and energy levels. Stay hydrated and limit alcohol.

Social Connection

Maintain relationships with supportive people. Share your feelings with trusted friends or family.

How to Help Someone

Start a Conversation

Approach with care and concern. Let them know you care and are there to listen without judgment.

Listen Actively

Let them express their feelings. Don't interrupt or offer quick solutions. Validate their feelings.

Connect to Resources

Help them find professional support. Offer to help make calls or accompany them to appointments.

Safety First

If you believe someone is in immediate danger, don't leave them alone. Call emergency services.

Remember: Seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Mental health challenges affect millions of people worldwide. Recovery is possible with proper support and treatment.
